Welcome

A working menu,not a contract.

Three packages, three monthly Care Plans, and a small list of à la carte add-ons. Three columns of pricing on every line, because the rate I charge depends on who you are to me.

Family

For people I owe my life to.

Spouses, parents, siblings, oldest friends. Lowest rate I can sustain. Locked in for the duration of our work together.

Friend

For people I love.

Folks I've broken bread with. A meaningful discount off Standard, in recognition of the relationship. Also locked in.

Standard

For everyone else.

The market rate I'd offer a stranger walking in cold. What I quote referrals (with a small "referred by" courtesy off the top).

A few notes before you read further. Family and Friend rates aren't transferable — anyone you refer pays Standard. All packages include a 30-day "if I haven't delivered what I promised, you don't pay" guarantee. A 50% deposit kicks off any project; balance due on launch. Care Plans are month-to-month, cancel anytime.

RK 05 · How this works
How this works

The fine print, without the fine print.

  1. Family & Friend rates are personal.

    They're locked in for the duration of our work together — meaning I won't quietly raise them on you. They aren't transferable to people you refer; anyone you send my way pays the Standard rate (with a small "referred by you" courtesy discount on top).

  2. Referral kickback.

    Every paying client you send me earns you a free month of your current Care Plan. Three referrals is three months of your site fully covered. Six is half a year. It's how I say thank you for the trust.

  3. The 30-day guarantee.

    If at the end of your project I haven't delivered what I promised, you don't pay the balance. No legal-speak, no escape hatches. I'd rather eat the loss than have your name attached to work I'm not proud of.

  4. Payments & timing.

    50% deposit kicks off any package; balance due on launch. Care Plans are month-to-month, cancel any time with 14 days notice. Hourly work and add-ons are invoiced after delivery. I send invoices through Stripe — credit card, ACH, all the usual.

  5. Ownership.

    You own your domain, your content, your site, and your brand assets. I keep the right to mention the project in my portfolio and case studies, unless you'd rather I didn't.
